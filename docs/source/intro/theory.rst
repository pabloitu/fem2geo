1. Mechanical models
-------------------

1.1 Mechanical models allow to calculate the evolution of stress and deformation fields throughout a synthetic modeled mesh domain. ADELI (Chéry & Hassany, 1997; Gerbault et al., 2018) is a Finite Element code that is based on the Dynamic relaxation method; it resolves the equation of motion in a quasi-static mode in materials that can behave elasto-visco-plastic.

1.2 Other thermomechanical models: FENICS, Pylith, ANSYS, etc.

2. Slip and Dilation Tendencies
----------------------------

2.1 Plotting slip and dilation tendencies out from a domain in which the stress tensor is available is a tool to evaluate how measuerd fractures and faults on a field area are consistent with that a priori stress field (e.g. Ritz et al, 1995).
It has been widely used in the Hazards and Geothermal comunities for decaddes (eg. Jolie et al., 2016).
